2 Calixto Card-Based Non-Player Rules

C8.1 Introduction & Components
Calixto is an updated Non-Player (NP) system, replacing the
system included in the Cuba Libre base game. A deck of cards
and several player aid tables replace the flowcharts ofthe original
Non-player Factions. Calixto is named in reference to the general
Calixto Garcia ffiiguez, an important leader in three early Cuban
rebellions against Spanish imperialism.

Important — When playing Cuba Libre with any NP Factions,

use this rulebook instead of section 8.0 in the original rules. |
|

C8.1.1 Components

* Calixto Cards. Each NP Faction has six Calixto cards that
provide instructions for selecting and executing Operations and
Special Activities.

Non-Player Game Aids. There are three Non-Player aid sheets:
one foldout aid containing Priorities, Eligibility, and Event tables
for all four NP Factions; one single sheet aid containing Event
Instructions on both sides; and one single sheet aid containing
Propaganda Round Instructions on one side, and a reminder of
the Golden Rules on the reverse side.

Priorities Tables. Calixto includes three types of Priorities
tables: Space Selection, Move, and Pieces. Each Faction uses
its own Space Selection Priorities table. All Factions share the
Move Priorities and Piece Priorities tables.

Eligibility Table. The Eligibility table determines how an Eli-
gible NP Faction will act on the Current Event card.

Event Tables. There are four Event tables: Event Cards, Effec-
tive Events, Event Instructions, and Additional Capability &
Momentum Instructions.

Propaganda Round Instructions. The Propaganda Round
Instructions guide the actions of each NP Faction during Pro-
paganda Rounds.

C8.2 Golden Rules

C8.2.1 Golden Rules
+ NP Factions Follow the Rules (Almost). NP Factions abide by
all applicable Cuba Libre rules, with these exceptions:
© NP Factions never remove pieces from the map to Available
when lacking pieces for an Operation, Special Activity, or
Event (1.4.1).
© NP Government, NP 26J, and NP Directorio do not track
Resources. Ignore any instructions to increase or decrease
Resources for these NP Factions. If an Operation, Special
Activity, or Event would transfer Resources between one
of these NP Factions and a player Faction or NP Syndicate,
the player Faction or NP Syndicate will just gain or lose the
indicated number of Resources.
© NP Syndicate tracks and spends Resources in the same way as
a player Faction. Aid should be tracked even if Government
is an NP Faction, as it can have an effect on some Events.

© NP Factions roll against an Activation Number to limit total
spaces selected (C8.5.4).

Skip Illegal Instructions. Calixto will only tell you what to do, it
will not tell you how to do it or even ifits instructions constitute
a legal move given the state of the board and available pieces.
Always carry out Calixto’s instructions as much as possible, but
if Calixto ever gives you an instruction that cannot be carried
out legally, just skip it.

Implement All Instructions Literally. Do not infer more to an
instruction than what the text actually specifies.

Placing Friendly Pieces. When executing Operations, Special
Activities, or Events that allow the acting Faction to place pieces
in multiple spaces, NP Factions place pieces one at a time. In
such cases, select the destination for each piece one at a time,
using the NP Faction’s Space Selection Priorities table. During
Train and Rally Operations, NP Factions will place as many
pieces as possible, following the directions on the Calixto card
and the limits of the given Operation. If multiple types of pieces
could be placed, determine the pieces to be placed using the
Piece Priorities table.

Removing Friendly Pieces. When given a choice, NP Factions
remove pieces one at a time, from the space with the most pieces
belonging to the affected NP Faction. Use the Piece Priorities
table to select pieces to remove from each selected space reading
from the bottom up and right to left.

Operation and Special Activity to the Maximum Extent. NP
Factions always execute a selected Operation or Special Activity
to the maximum extent allowed, given the state of the board,
available pieces, and Activation Number die rolls.

Using Cash. NP Government, NP 26J, and NP Directorio will
always use held Cash markers for a free Limited Operation at
the first opportunity, i.e., when they next perform an Operation
Only or Limited Operation. Draw an additional Calixto card to
execute this free Limited Operation. NP SYN will never do so.
Removing Cash. When forced to remove Cash, NP Factions
will pass it to another of their pieces in the same space if possi-
ble, then to another NP Faction in the same space, in the order
NP Government, NP Directorio, NP 26J, NP Syndicate. If no
other NP Faction can receive the Cash then they will remove
it to Available.

When in Doubt, Randomize. Whenever NP Factions need to
select from among several options and supplied instructions have
been exhausted, select one option at random.

Important Calixto Terms

1d6, 2d6, 3d6, 1d3

Die or dice roll to generate a random number. 1d6 means roll
the die once; 2d6 means sum two dice, etc. 1d3 means roll one
die and halve the result, rounding up.

Activation Number (AN)

A limit on the total number of spaces selected by NP Factions for
most Operations. When present, an NP Faction must roll greater
than its Activation Number to select another space (C8.5.4).

Effective Event
An Event is Effective if one or more of its effects are listed for
that NP Faction on the Effective Events table (C8.5.5).

Enemy Forces

All other forces that do not belong to the acting NP Faction:
cubes, Guerrillas, Bases, and Open Casinos. Ignore Closed
Casinos when checking for enemy forces.

Get

Move just enough pieces to the destination to meet the instruc-
tion, taking into account any pieces already present in the space.
If no additional pieces are needed to meet the instruction, move
none. If there are insufficient pieces to meet the instruction,
move as many as possible. When a ‘get’ instruction might
involve several spaces, select the one where it is possible to
accomplish as much of the instruction as possible.

Keep

Leave just enough pieces in the origin to meet the instruction.
If there are insufficient pieces to meet a particular instruction,
ignore it.

Most/Least Support/Opposition

Among all spaces with Support, spaces with the most Support
are those with the most Total Support (1.6.3). Spaces with the
least Support are those with no Support first (Neutral and Oppo-
sition spaces), then spaces with the least Total Support. Among
all spaces with Opposition, spaces with the most Opposition
are those with the most Total Opposition. Spaces with the least
Opposition are those with no Opposition first (Neutral and
Support spaces), then spaces with the least Total Opposition.

Within Reach

A space is within reach of moving pieces if those pieces could
move to that space during the selected Operation or Special
Activity.

C8.3 Victory
C8.3.1 Playing Solitaire

When playing solitaire against Calixto, the player may only win
during the final Propaganda Round. NP Factions may win during
the Victory Phase of any Propaganda Round.

C8.3.2 Playing With Two or Three Players
When playing with two or three players, any Faction may win
during any Propaganda Round.

C8.4 Setup
C8.4.1 Setting Up NP Factions

* Create the Calixto Deck. Each NP faction will use six Calixto
cards marked with their Faction color and symbol. Shuffle the
cards for all NP Factions being used into one deck, face up.

* Resource Cylinders. Set aside NP Government, NP 26J, and/
or NP Directorio Resource cylinders. NP Syndicate is the only
NP Faction that will track Resources.

C8.5 Sequence of Play

C8.5.1 Overview

Generally, NP Factions make the same decisions that player Fac-
tions do. First, Eligible NP Factions determine if and how they
will act on the current Event card. The Calixto card deck is used
to select Operations and Special Activities and provide instructions
to execute that action. Event tables guide an NP Faction’s selection
and execution of Events. Priorities tables are used to select spaces
where NP Factions will act, how NP Factions move pieces, and
which friendly and enemy pieces are affected by an action.

NP Factions choose from the same options as player Factions. Use

an Operation plus Special Activity, Operation Only, or Limited
Operation, use the active NP Faction’s Calixto cards and the

Activities with Calixto Cards

Each NP Faction has six Calixto cards that provide instructions for
selecting and executing Operations and Special Activities (skip any
Special Activity instructions if performing a Limited Operation or
Operation without Special Activity).

* Face Up and Face Down. Face up sides are identified by a a
icon at center top. Except when acting as the active card, Calixto
cards should be face up.

Setting Up the Calixto Deck. At the start of the game and during
the Reset Phase of each Propaganda Round, shuffle together all
Calixto cards for NP Factions into a single deck, face up. These
are the only times that the deck is shuffled.

Drawing Calixto Cards. First, move the top card to the bottom
of the deck (even if this top card matches the active NP Faction).
Continue moving cards to the bottom, one at a time, until a card
matching the active NP Faction is the top card. Always place
cards on the bottom of the deck face up. The played card then
remains on the top of the deck until a new card needs to be drawn.
Reading Calixto Cards. Start with the first blue box at the top
of the card. If the condition in the box is true, follow the green
arrow; if it is false, follow the red arrow.

Drawing a New Card and Flipping a Card. [fan arrow points
to the & icon, draw a new card. If an arrow points to the ©]
icon, flip the card to its face down side.

Executing Operations. Execute each numbered instruction in
order, as much as possible (i.e., execute instruction @ inas many
spaces as possible, then @, and so on — remembering that NP
Factions follow all of the usual rules about space selection, etc.).
Follow the instructions on the Calixto card to select spaces, using
the active NP Faction’s Space Selection Priorities table when
directed. Text in bold specifies the column of that NP Faction’s

Space Selection Priorities table to use. Arrowed instructions
serve as reminders for the implementation of the preceding
instruction. Starred instructions are executed immediately when
they are reached on a card, and do not require any Activation
Number rolls (C8.5.4). Instructions that follow a red conditional
are only executed if that condition is true. If there are no legal
spaces for the selected Operation, draw a new Calixto card te
select a different Operation. See C8.6 Operations Reference for
detailed instructions of each Operation.

the list, selecting the first possible in letter order (i.e., select a

Special Activity. Within each Special Activity, execute each
numbered instruction in order, as much as possible (i.e., execute
instruction @ in as many spaces as possible, then ©. and o
on — remembering that NP Factions follow all of the usual rules
about space selection, etc.). Text in bold specifies the column ef
the Space Selection Priorities table to use when selecting spaces
if multiple spaces could be selected within the instructions giy-

red condition is not true, skip it and execute the next one. If ae
Special Activity can be executed, select either Operation Only
(if Ist Eligible), or select Operation and Special Activity without
performing any Special Activity. See C8.7 Special Activities
Reference for detailed instructions of each Special Activity.

Important — Remember not to execute a Special Activity if
the NP Faction is performing a Limited Operation!

Loops and Edge Cases. Before executing any Special Activity
for an NP Faction, check that the accompanying Operation cam

C8.5.5 Executing Events

+ Event Tables. There are four Event tables: Event Cards, Effec-
tive Events, Event Instructions, and Additional Capability &
Momentum Instructions. The Event Cards table lists whether
each Event is Critical, Performed, or not executed for each NP
Faction, and notes if there are additional instructions for an Event
on the Event Instructions table. The Effective Events table lists
the types of Event actions that are effective. When given a choice,
NP Factions only execute actions that are effective. The Event
Instructions table gives specific instructions for executing certain
Events. The Additional Capability & Momentum Instructions
table, found on the back of this rulebook, specifies the effects of
certain Capability and Momentum cards that are in play (most
are self-explanatory and do not require any additional notes).
Events Executed by Ist and 2nd Eligible NP Factions. A Ist
Eligible NP Faction will execute an Event if it is marked Critical
for the acting NP Faction and, if executed, would result in one
or more of the effects listed for the acting NP Faction on the
Effective Events table. A 2nd Eligible NP Faction will execute
an Event if it is marked either Critical or Performed for the
acting NP Faction and, if executed, would result in one or more
of the effects listed for the acting NP Faction on the Effective
Events table.

Event Instructions. Some Events require additional instructions
to guide NP Faction play. These Events are marked with a *
on the Event Cards table. See the Event Instructions table for
additional instructions.

* Unshaded or Shaded? Unless otherwise noted on the Event
Instructions table, NP Government and NP Syndicate will always
select the shaded Event text, and NP 26J and NP Directorio will
always select the unshaded Event text.

Place Friendly, Remove Enemy. When given a choice, NP Fac-
tions only place friendly pieces and only remove enemy pieces.
Selecting Spaces. Select spaces using the column of the active
NP Faction’s Space Selection Priorities that matches the effec-
tive Event action. If the Event includes more than one effective
action, use the leftmost matching column.

Events that allow Operations or Special Activities. Draw a
Calixto card for the active NP Faction to select an Operation or
Special Activity. If the Event specifies the Operation or Special
Activity, draw Calixto cards until a matching Operation or Spe-
cial Activity is drawn (check both sides).

Faction Specific Reference: See C8.8 Events Reference for NP
Faction-specific guidelines.

C8.5.6 Space Selection Priorities Tables

There are four Space Selection Priorities tables, one for each NP
Faction. Use an NP Faction’s Space Selection Priorities table to
select spaces for Operations, Special Activities, Events, and during
Propaganda Rounds. First determine the type of action the NP
Faction will execute to determine the column to use on the Space
Selection Priorities table. During Operations and Special Activi-
ties, use the column specified by the bold text on the Calixto card.
During Events, use the column that matches the effective action
specified in the Event text. If an Event specifies more than one type
of effective action, use the leftmost column on the Space Selection
Priorities table. During Propaganda Rounds, follow the instructions
on the Propaganda Round Instructions sheet.

* Selecting a Space. When executing an Operation, Special Ac-

tivity, or Event, select each space one at a time:

Step 1. Start at the top of the column and read down. For each
row with a dot in that column, check its condition
against all spaces that could be selected. Some rows
contain conditional statements, marked by a red check-
mark, that must be true for a red dot to apply. If no
spaces meet the condition, skip that row and continue
to the next. If only one space meets this condition,
execute the Operation, Special Activity, or Event in
this space. Otherwise,

Step 2. If more than one space meets this condition, continue
down the column to the next condition that is met.
Continue to skip conditions that are not met or whose
red conditional statement is not true. If only one space
meets both the original condition and the new condi-
tion, execute the action in this space (as in Step 1).

Step 3. Continue this process, checking each row below and
applying all meeting conditions, until a single space
is selected.

Step 4. Repeat this process from Step 1 for each space to be
selected.

C8.5.7 Move Priorities Table

Whenever an NP Faction needs to move its pieces on the map,
use the Move Priorities table. Read down the column that matches
the active NP Faction, executing each instruction in a dotted row.
Dotted rows with a red conditional only apply if the condition is
true. See C8.6 Operations Reference and C8.7 Special Activities
Reference for detailed instructions of Operations and Special
Activities that move pieces.

Step A. Select a destination space using the column of the active
NP Faction’s Space Selection Priorities table that matches the
selected Operation or Special Activity.

Step B. Select an origin space (not already selected as a des-
tination) within reach of the destination space with the most
moveable pieces belonging to the acting NP Faction. Use the
Move Priorities table (C8.5.7) to check which pieces will be kept
(and are therefore not moveable) in each possible origin space.
Keep Pieces in Origin. Reading down the column that match-
es the active NP Faction, keep pieces in the selected origin as
instructed by each dotted row. Instructions that follow a red
conditional are only executed if that condition is true.

Get Pieces to Destination. Reading down the column that
matches the active NP Faction, move pieces into the selected
destination as instructed by each dotted row. Instructions that
follow a red conditional are only executed if that condition

is true. If an NP Faction would move no forces to a selected
destination, return to Step A and select a new destination space
(without having to make an AN roll).

Important — Only move pieces as necessary to fulfill the
acting NP Faction’s ‘get’ instructions on the Move Priorities
table. This may sometimes mean that no pieces are moved,
in which case you should skip straight to selecting a new
destination space.

Step C. After all moving pieces have moved from the first origin,
check to see if additional origins or destinations will be selected.
To select a new destination, return to Step A. To select a new
origin, return to Step B. Note that the NP Faction may need to
pass an Activation Number roll to select additional destination
spaces during Operations (C8.5.4).

Important — Unless otherwise specified by the current
Calixto card or Move Priorities table instructions, select
moving pieces using the Piece Priorities table (within the
constraints of the Operation, Special Activity, or Event being
executed).

C8.5.8 Piece Priorities Table

When an NP Faction needs to select pieces from among several
different types, use the Piece Priorities table. Always remove, re-
place, or Activate player pieces before NP pieces. When placing,
moving, or using friendly pieces or moving, removing, replacing,
or Activating enemy pieces, start from the top and read down;
within boxes, read left to right. When removing friendly pieces,
start from the bottom and read up; within boxes, read right to left.

C8.6.2 Executing an Operation using Calixto
Cards

Follow all Base Game Rules. Except for Resources (C8.5.4),
NP Factions follow all base game rules for Operations.
Numbered Instructions First. Execute each Operation num-
bered instruction in order as much as possible before moving
on to the next instruction. If an instruction cannot be executed,
skip it.

Selecting Spaces. Use the active NP Faction’s Space Selection
Priorities table to select spaces for the Operation.

Operations that Move Pieces. Use the Move Priorities table to
execute Sweep, Garrison, and March Operations.

Red Conditionals. Only implement an instruction following a
red conditional if that condition is true.

Arrowed Notes. Some instructions are followed by notes marked
with an arrow; these serve as reminders for the execution of the
preceding instruction.

Activation Numbers. After executing the Operation in each
selected space, make an Activation Number roll (C8.5.4), un-
less this Operation is one of the exceptions (C8.5.4). Once the
Activation Number roll has failed, select no additional spaces
for the Operation. Do not make an Activation Number roll after
executing instructions numbered by a white circle.

Starred Instructions Last. Execute any starred instructions
once all allowed spaces have been selected for the Operation.
Syndicate Resources. Spend Syndicate Resources for NP Syn-
dicate Operations as if a human player.

C8.6.3 Capability & Momentum Instructions
See the Additional Capability & Momentum Instructions table
(located on the back page of this rulebook) for the effects of certain

all such cards are listed here, as most effects are self-explanatory
and do not require any additional NP instructions.

C8.6.4 NP Government Operations

NP Government executes each Operation as much as possible,
given available pieces and the state of the board. Follow all pro-
cedures for the selected Operation. Execute the selected Operation
as much as possible.

Follow the directions for each instruction, in the order given by
the NP Government Calixto card.

NP Government Train Procedures:

Place cubes, in sets of 4 only. Use the Place Cubes column of
the NP Government Space Selection Priorities table to select
spaces. Per the Piece Priorities table, alternate placing one
Police then one Troop. Only place cubes in a selected space
if it is possible to place exactly four.

After all cubes have been placed (if any), perform the first
applicable of the following instructions (regardless of whether
the Operation’s Activation Number roll has failed or not, and
even if no spaces were selected to place pieces):

Shift Toward Active Support. Use the Shift Toward Active
Support column to select one space that meets the criteria for
Civic Action, then remove Terror and shift it towards Support
for a total of 1d3 markers removed/shifts. Unless this is a
Limited Operation, the space selected for Civic Action could
be an additional one that was not selected for Train, with no
Activation Number roll required to select the space (if it is a
Limited Operation, only the single space selected for Train
may be shifted towards Support).

Place Base. Replace two cubes (one Police and one Troop
if possible) with one Government Base. Unless this is a
Limited Operation, the space selected to place a Base could
be an additional one that was not selected for Train, with no
Activation Number roll required to select the space (if it is a
Limited Operation, only the single space selected for Train
could be selected to place a Base).

NP Government Garrison Procedures:

Use Move Priorities. Use the Garrison column of the NP Gov-
ernment Space Selection Priorities table to select destinations;
and the Move Priorities table to select origins, pieces to keep
in selected origins, and pieces to move into selected destina-
tions. Use the Remove column of the NP Government Space
Selection Priorities table to select one eligible EC for Assault.

NP Government Sweep Procedures:

Use Move Priorities. Use the Sweep column of the NP Gov-
ernment Space Selection Priorities table to select destinations;
and the Move Priorities table to select origins, pieces to keep
in selected origins, and pieces to move into selected destina-
tions. NP Government will Sweep in selected destinations
with Underground Guerrillas even if they do not move any
Troops there. In each destination, Activate any Underground
Guerrillas per 3.2.3, after all moving pieces have entered

C8.7.1 Selecting a Special Activity

* Use the Current Calixto Card. The Calixto card and side that

Red Conditionals. Only implementa Special Activity or instruc-

Follow all Base Game Rules. NP Factions follow all base game
rules for Special Activities.

Numbered Instructions First. Execute each Special Activ-
ity numbered instruction in order as much as possible before
moving on to the next instruction. If an instruction cannot be
executed, skip it.

Priorities table to select spaces for the Special Activity.
Special Activities that Move Pieces. Use the Move Priorities
table to execute Transport Special Activities.

Red Conditionals. Only implement an instruction following a
red conditional if that condition is true.

Starred Instructions Last. Execute any starred instructions once
all allowed spaces have been selected for the Special Activity.

Designer Notes

When I was first asked to design the Jacquard system for Cuba
Libre, I had three main areas of concern to address. First, the overall
design: it’s been said that Cuba Libre is a fist fight in a phone booth,
and I wanted the cards to be able to reflect the back-and-forth nature
and large swings between winning and falling behind. Secondly,
the Syndicate faction posed an interesting problem compared to
other volumes in that resource tracking, traditionally something
ignored by NP Jacquard factions, was inherently part of their win
condition. And lastly, there is less freedom between Operation and
Special Activity combinations.

The first concern turned out to be quite the benefit. Much as a
human player does, the NP factions in Calixto can change strategy
to respond to more immediate threats without losing too much
momentum. The cards are designed to be more responsive to on-
map elements. However, each faction was also designed with an
overall goal in mind. For example, the Directorio wants to put out
as many pieces as possible in order to reposition them for control,
so their cards will heavily feature Rally Operations until they have
enough Guerrillas to start Marching.

When it comes to the Syndicate, I tried a few options for tracking
Resources and Open Casinos. The first draft tracked Resources
just as a human player would. We then toyed with the alternative
of a new counter to track how many Casinos could be built. While
maybe slightly cleaner — there are no odd numbers of resources
to worry about — this was effectively the same as having to track
them anyways, so I tried to not track them at all. However, this
ran into a problem very quickly of how to design the cards with
appropriate Activation Numbers for Construct. Ultimately, it was
decided that just keeping the same Syndicate Resource tracking for
Construct and for Cash income was the simplest. Most players of
Calixto would be familiar with this already from playing the base
game, and the added fidelity was worth the cost of including the
slight extra overhead of tracking Syndicate Resources.

Lastly, the combinations of Operation and Special Activities are
far fewer than we had to play with when designing Jacquard bots
for Fire in the Lake. This meant that, unfortunately, designing
the cards from an Operation-first perspective resulted in fewer
branching paths. This was a high-level concern I kept an eye on
during the entire testing process, but it didn’t seem to cause any
strategic or competitive issues. In cases where the bot goes first,
this can sometimes block a player from getting more than a Limited
Operation, and in cases where the bot goes second, sometimes
an Operation only — the bread-and-butter of Cuba Libre — is fine
enough to get an edge over a human player.

All in all, I’m very happy with what the development team has
put together and I find the Calixto bots to be very competitive and
strategic. We hope you enjoy playing against them!

Kevin Crooks
Colorado, USA
